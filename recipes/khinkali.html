what is khinkali?

Khinkali are Georgian dumplings that are a favorite at dinner parties or supras. The most common version of them is meat-filled, like in this recipe. In addition to the meat, the dumplings have a brothy filling due to the juices that the raw ground meat produces when cooked.

While the soup filling is similar to Chinese soup dumplings, the dough is thicker and they are boiled, instead of steamed.

dough

  • 500 g flour
  • 250 ml water
  • 1 tsp salt

filling

  • 500 g ground beef, pork, or a mixture
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2–3 cloves garlic, minced (optional)
  • Fresh cilantro and parsley, chopped
  • 1 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 150–200 ml cold water or broth

instructions

Make the Dough

  1. Mix flour and salt in a bowl
  2. Gradually add water and knead until smooth
  3. Knead for about 10 minutes.
  4. Cover and let it rest for 30 minutes

Prepare the Filling

  1. Mix the meat, onion, herbs, salt, and pepper.
  2. Slowly add cold water or broth while mixing. The filling should be juicy but not watery

Shape the Khinkali

  1. Roll the dough to about 2–3 mm thick
  2. Cut circles about 10–12 cm wide
  3. Place a spoonful of filling in the center of each circle
  4. Gather the edges into pleats and twist the top closed

Cook

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a gentle boil
  2. Carefully add the khinkali
  3. Stir once so they don't stick
  4. Cook for 10–12 minutes after they float to the surface
