what is imeruli khachapuri?

Imeruli Khachapuri is one of the most popular traditional Georgian dishes. It is a round bread filled with cheese and baked until golden brown.

Originating from the Imereti region of Georgia, it is known for its soft dough and rich cheese filling. It is commonly served warm and enjoyed as a meal or snack.

dough

  • 500 g flour
  • 250 ml warm water
  • 7 g dry yeast
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil

filling

  • 500 g imeruli cheese or a mixture of feta and mozzarella
  • 1 egg (optional)

instructions

Make the Dough

  1. Mix the warm water, sugar, and yeast in a bowl
  2. Let it sit for 5–10 minutes until foamy
  3. Add the flour, salt, and oil
  4. Knead until smooth and elastic
  5. Cover and let it rise for 1 hour

Prepare the Filling

  1. Grate or crumble the cheese into a bowl
  2. Mix with the egg if using

Shape the Khachapuri

  1. Roll the dough into a large circle
  2. Place the cheese filling in the center
  3. Gather the edges and seal the filling inside
  4. Flatten gently into a round shape

Cook

  1. Preheat the oven to 220°C
  2. Place the khachapuri on a baking tray
  3. Bake for 15–20 minutes until golden brown
  4. Remove from the oven and serve warm
