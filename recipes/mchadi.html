what is mchadi?

Mchadi is a traditional Georgian cornbread made primarily from cornmeal, water, and salt. It is a staple food in many regions of Georgia and is often served alongside beans, cheese, or various meat dishes.

Mchadi has a crispy golden crust on the outside and a soft texture inside. It is usually pan-fried, although it can also be baked.

ingredients

  • 500 g cornmeal
  • 350 ml warm water
  • 1 tsp salt
  • Oil for frying

instructions

Prepare the Dough

  1. Mix the cornmeal and salt in a bowl
  2. Gradually add the warm water while mixing
  3. Knead until a soft dough forms
  4. Let the dough rest for 10 minutes

Shape the Mchadi

  1. Divide the dough into equal portions
  2. Shape each portion into a small round patty
  3. Flatten slightly with your hands

Cook

  1. Heat a small amount of oil in a frying pan
  2. Place the mchadi in the pan
  3. Cook for 4–5 minutes on each side until golden brown
  4. Remove and serve warm

how to eat mchadi

  1. Serve the mchadi warm
  2. Break off a small piece with your hands
  3. Enjoy it on its own or with cheese, beans, or butter
  4. Continue breaking off pieces and eating until finished
